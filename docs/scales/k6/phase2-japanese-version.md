## Translation Method

✅ **Back-Translation Method**: Yes
- Original English version → Japanese translation → Back-translation to English
- Comparison with original version to ensure semantic equivalence
- Expert panel review (psychiatrists and psychologists)
- Cultural adaptation for Japanese context

### Translation Quality:
The Japanese version maintained the conceptual equivalence of the original scale while ensuring cultural appropriateness for Japanese respondents.

## Study Design (Furukawa et al., 2008)

### Sample:
- **N**: 2,436 participants
- **Setting**: World Mental Health Survey Japan (WMH-J)
- **Coverage**: 7 communities across Japan
- **Type**: Psychiatric epidemiologic study
- **Gold Standard**: DSM-IV disorders via CIDI (Composite International Diagnostic Interview)

## Psychometric Properties (Japanese Version)

### Internal Consistency:
- **K6 Cronbach's α**: 0.89
- **K10 Cronbach's α**: 0.93
- **Comparison with Original**: Equivalent (original α = 0.89)
- **Δα**: 0.00 (identical)

### Screening Performance:
- **AUC**: 0.94 (95% CI: 0.88-0.99)
- **Target**: DSM-IV mood and anxiety disorders
- **Interpretation**: Excellent discrimination ability

### Comparative Performance:
"The Japanese versions of the K6 and K10 demonstrated screening performances **essentially equivalent** to those of the original English versions."

### Sensitivity/Specificity:
The optimal cutoff values and operating characteristics were determined using ROC analysis against structured diagnostic interviews (CIDI).

## Factor Structure

### Confirmatory Factor Analysis:
The Japanese K6 maintained a single-factor structure consistent with the original scale, representing a unidimensional construct of non-specific psychological distress.

## National Adoption

✅ **Government Use**: K6 has been adopted in Japan's **National Livelihood Survey (国民生活基礎調査)** conducted by the Ministry of Health, Labour and Welfare (厚生労働省).

### Public Health Implementation:
- Used nationwide for population mental health monitoring
- Standardized administration across all prefectures
- Regular data collection for epidemiological trends
- Published versions available from multiple health centers (Chiyoda Ward, Shimane Prefecture, etc.)

## Question Items (Japanese Version)

### Instruction:
**過去30日の間に、どれくらいの頻度で次のことがありましたか。**

### 6 Items:

1. 神経過敏に感じましたか
2. 絶望的だと感じましたか
3. そわそわ、落ち着かなく感じましたか
4. 気分が沈み込んで、何が起こっても気が晴れないように感じましたか
5. 何をするのも骨折りだと感じましたか
6. 自分は価値のない人間だと感じましたか

### Response Options:
- まったくない (0点)
- 少しだけ (1点)
- ときどき (2点)
- たいてい (3点)
- いつも (4点)

## Scoring (Japanese Version)

### Total Score Range: 0-24

### Severity Categories:
- **0-4点**: 問題なし (No distress)
- **5-9点**: 軽度の心理的苦痛 (Mild distress)
- **10-12点**: 中等度の心理的苦痛 (Moderate distress)
- **13-24点**: 重度の心理的苦痛 (Severe distress)

### Clinical Cutoff:
**≥ 13点**: High probability of serious mental illness (精神疾患の可能性が高い)

### Interpretation Notes:
- **10点以上**: 要注意 - 休息や娯楽を大切にしてください
- **13点以上**: 日常生活に支障が出ているかも - 医師や心理士などの専門家に相談することをお勧めします
