# Phase 1 Report: Industriousness (勤勉性)

## Basic Information

- **Full Name**: Industriousness (Big Five Aspect Scale)
- **Alternative Names**:
  - Achievement Striving + Self-Discipline (IPIP-NEO facets)
  - Work Ethic
  - Diligence
- **Developer**: DeYoung, C. G., Quilty, L. C., & Peterson, J. B.
- **Year**: 2007
- **Journal**: Journal of Personality and Social Psychology, 93(5), 880-896
- **DOI**: 10.1037/0022-3514.93.5.880
- **Citations**: 3,500+ (Google Scholar, as of 2026)

## Scale Overview

Industriousness is one of the **two aspects of Conscientiousness** in the Big Five Aspect Scale (BFAS). While Conscientiousness as a whole reflects orderliness and goal-directed behavior, it can be decomposed into:

1. **Industriousness**: Motivation for achievement, work ethic, persistence in goal pursuit
2. **Orderliness**: Preference for order, organization, and structure

## Measurement Approach

### BFAS Industriousness (Original)
- **Items**: 10 items
- **Response Format**: 5-point Likert scale (1 = Very Inaccurate, 5 = Very Accurate)
- **Score Range**: 10-50
- **Source**: IPIP (International Personality Item Pool)

### IPIP-300 Composition (Alternative)
Industriousness can also be measured using two NEO-PI-R facets from IPIP-300:

- **C4: Achievement Striving** (達成動機)
  - 10 items
  - Measures: Goal-setting, ambition, drive to succeed
  - Cronbach's α = 0.79

- **C5: Self-Discipline** (自己鍛錬)
  - 10 items
  - Measures: Task initiation, follow-through, resistance to procrastination
  - Cronbach's α = 0.85

**Combined C4+C5 (20 items)**:
- Cronbach's α (estimated) = 0.82
- This combination aligns closely with BFAS Industriousness

## Psychometric Properties

### BFAS Industriousness
- **Items**: 10
- **Cronbach's α**: 0.81 (excellent internal consistency)
- **Test-Retest**: Not reported in original paper, but BFAS scales show good temporal stability
- **Sample Size**:
  - Eugene-Springfield Community Sample (ESCS): n = 481
  - University Sample: n = 487
  - Retest Sample: n = 90

### IPIP-300 C4+C5 Combined
- **Items**: 20 (10 + 10)
- **Cronbach's α**:
  - C4: 0.79
  - C5: 0.85
  - Combined: ~0.82 (estimated)
- **Source**: Johnson (2014), IPIP official comparison table
- **Sample Size**: Large internet samples (n > 300,000)

### Convergent Validity
- **Correlation with Conscientiousness domain**: r ≈ 0.85-0.90
- **Correlation with Grit (Perseverance of Effort)**: r = 0.77-0.92 (Schmidt et al., 2018)
- **Distinguishability from Orderliness**: r = 0.40-0.50 (moderate, indicating distinct subdomains)
- **Academic Performance**: Positive predictor of GPA, task completion
- **Work Performance**: Positive predictor of job performance, especially in achievement contexts

## Psychological Layer Classification

**Trait (特性)** - 性格特性

**Category**: Personality → Conscientiousness subdomain

**Justification**:
- Relatively stable over time
- Genetically influenced (heritability ≈ 0.40-0.50)
- Part of the Big Five personality structure
- Not a transient state or outcome
- While potentially modifiable through sustained effort, primarily reflects stable individual differences

**Note on Trait vs. Skill Debate**:
While Industriousness involves behaviors that *can* be trained (e.g., time management, goal-setting), research indicates it primarily reflects stable personality traits rather than learned skills. This is evidenced by:
- Genetic correlations (r = 0.86 with Conscientiousness)
- Temporal stability across lifespan
- Cross-situational consistency

However, for **user experience** purposes, emphasizing the "improvable" aspects in result interpretations can be motivating while remaining scientifically accurate.

## Key Research Findings

### What Industriousness Measures

**Core Components**:
1. **Achievement Motivation**: Drive to accomplish goals, set high standards
2. **Work Ethic**: Willingness to work hard and persist
3. **Task Persistence**: Ability to maintain effort despite obstacles
4. **Goal-Directedness**: Focus on long-term objectives

**Distinguishing Features**:
- **vs. Orderliness**: Industriousness = goal pursuit; Orderliness = organization
- **vs. Grit**: Industriousness = trait; Grit = narrow construct (jangle fallacy debate)
- **vs. Self-Control**: Broader than impulse control; includes proactive achievement

### Practical Implications

**High Industriousness**:
- Strong academic and work performance
- Goal achievement and career success
- Risk: Workaholism, burnout, perfectionism

**Low Industriousness**:
- More relaxed approach to goals
- Flexible, adaptable to changing priorities
- Risk: Underachievement, procrastination
