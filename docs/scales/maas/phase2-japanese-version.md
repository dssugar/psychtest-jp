## Scale Format (Japanese Version)

- **Items**: 15 items (same as original)
- **Response Format**: 6-point Likert scale (6件法)
  - 1: ほとんど全くない (Almost Never)
  - 2: めったにない (Very Infrequently)
  - 3: たまにある (Somewhat Infrequently)
  - 4: ときどきある (Somewhat Frequently)
  - 5: しばしばある (Very Frequently)
  - 6: ほとんど常にある (Almost Always)

- **Scoring**: Total score = sum or mean of all items
  - Higher scores indicate higher mindfulness
  - All items are reverse-scored (measuring lack of mindfulness)

## Item Response Theory (IRT) Analysis

The study employed **Item Response Theory** analysis, which provided additional psychometric insights:

- **Discrimination Power**: The Japanese version could differentiate between low and very low levels of the latent trait (mindfulness)
- **Item Functioning**: IRT confirmed appropriate item characteristics for measuring mindfulness across the spectrum
